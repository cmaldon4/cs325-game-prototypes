Schwifty Pet Shop Updated

  • High Concept: This reaction based game involves quick reflexes to determine if an opening cage should stay open due to a customer purchase, or shut due to an animals escape attempt.
  • Theme: The theme used was pet shop owner.
  • Mandated Variety: The input used keyboard only, with a combination of deterministc and randomness. The play style is competitor and achiever, while the genre is racing/platformer.
  • Player Experience Goals: The purpose of this game is to test the playerâ€™s agility and response time to two different stimuli. The player should feel motivated, focused, and competitive with themselves and the game to keep their pet shop running smoothly.
  • Gameplay: The game starts off with a tutorial that must be completed before the game actually begins that walks through the core mechanics of the game. For this game, there is a 3x3 grid with animals in each cell. The animals do not change position. After the first customer comes in and requests an animal (approx 4 s after entering, the request shows up as a speech bubble with an image of an animal inside it that matches animal type and thinking sound effect will go off, but not exact image of the animals in the cages) the cell doors will start opening at random and potentially the same time. After 2 seconds have passed from the customer purchase request, the targeted animal cage will definitely open. The player must press the w, e, r, s, d, f, x, c, v or 7, 8, 9, 4, 5, 6, 1, 2, 3 numpad keys each matching the cells going from left to right, top to bottom respectively. Ex. w/7 matches top left cell, e/8 matches top middle and so forth. If the user presses the key to close the cage and prevent the animal from escaping and it isn't within 3 seconds of the cage door opening or if it should have been left open to give the customer that animal, a life is lost with money also being lost if a pet escapes. If a cage is left open that doesn't match the customer's request, a life is also lost and so is money lost. Whenever money is lost it is on the scale of 20 money units. A sound goes off every time a customer comes in (which is every 10 seconds) and the customer requests an animal after 4 seconds of entry. So a key press respective to a specific cage will shut the cage. If the cage of the desired animal is left open, then 50 money units are gained. If the customer is denied, they make an unhappy sound and a life is lost. The game can be won by earning 450 min. money units, or lost by losing all lives.
  • Prototype Goal: The game mechanic being evaluated is reaction based key matching accuracy.
  • Features: What is unique about this game is the used of nine different keys to achieve a general goal, forcing the user to keep track of a lot.
  • Setting/Premise: The premise is that there is a pet shop that customers love to shop at, but the animals are sneaky and are constantly trying to escape to their forever homes.
  • Story: I don't see an extended story for this game.
  • Target Audience: Target demographic would be agile quick reflexed people who are self competitive/game competitive.
  • Play Time: Anywhere from 0-infinity, but a few minutes on average.
  • Strategies: Practice reflexes, good hand eye coordination and memorization.
