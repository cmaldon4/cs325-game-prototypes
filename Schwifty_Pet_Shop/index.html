Schwifty Pet Shop

  • High Concept: This reaction based game involves quick reflexes to determine if an opening cage should stay open due to a customer purchase, or shut due to an animals escape attempt.
  • Theme: The theme used was pet shop owner.
  • Mandated Variety: The input used keyboard only, with a combination of deterministc and randomness. The play style is competitor and achiever, while the genre is racing/platformer.
  • Player Experience Goals: The purpose of this game is to test the playerâ€™s agility and response time to two different stimuli. The player should feel motivated, focused, and competitive with themselves and the game to keep their pet shop running smoothly.
  • Gameplay: For this game, there is a 3x3 grid with animals in each cell. The animals do not change position. After the first customer comes in and requests an animal (approx 4 s after entering) the cell doors will start opening at random and potentially the same time. After 2 seconds have passed from the customer purchase request, the targeted animal cage will definitely open. The player must press the w, e, r, s, d, f, x, c, v or 7, 8, 9, 4, 5, 6, 1, 2, 3 numpad keys each matching the cells going from left to right, top to bottom respectively. Ex. w/7 matches top left cell, e/8 matches top middle and so forth. If the user presses the key to close the cage and prevent the animal from escaping and it isn't within 3 seconds of the cage door opening or if it should have been left open to give the customer that animal, the game ends. If a cage is left open that doesn't match the customer's request, the game also ends. A sound goes off every time a customer comes in (which is every 10 seconds) and the customer requests an animal after 4 seconds of entry. So a key press respective to a specific cage will shut the cage.
  • Prototype Goal: The game mechanic being evaluated is reaction based key matching accuracy.
  • Features: What is unique about this game is the used of nine different keys to achieve a general goal, forcing the user to keep track of a lot.
  • Setting/Premise: The premise is that there is a pet shop that customers love to shop at, but the animals are sneaky and are constantly trying to escape to their forever homes.
